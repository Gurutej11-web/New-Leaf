
# New Leaf

**Youth Code x AI Hackathon 2026 — Track 03: AI That Actually Helps People**  

## What It Is

New Leaf is a conversational AI application that helps people in crisis find support in their community. Users describe their situation in natural language, and the AI generates a personalized step-by-step plan with relevant local resources, financial support options, and possible next steps.

Users can also be redirected toward government programs, official hotlines, nonprofit organizations, and other trusted community partners.

**One-line pitch:**  
An AI-powered solution that helps people in crisis access community support services and get personalized guidance so they can get help faster and advocate for themselves.

## The Problem

People in crisis often struggle to find the right support services because helpful information is scattered across many websites, agencies, and programs. When someone is already stressed, hungry, facing housing insecurity, or dealing with financial pressure, they may not know where to start.

For example, a student whose family is struggling to pay rent may not know what housing assistance, food assistance, or emergency aid programs are available nearby.

If this problem stays unsolved, people can miss urgent support such as food, housing, and financial assistance. This can worsen crises, increase stress, and create financial and emotional harm that could have been prevented if help were easier to find.

## Why This Fits Track 03

Track 03, **AI That Actually Helps People**, focuses on using AI to solve real community problems and build projects that make someone’s life better.

New Leaf fits this track because it acts as a local community helper. Instead of only giving general advice, it helps users understand their situation, organize their options, and find support services that may be relevant to their needs.

## Why AI

AI is useful for this problem because people do not always describe their needs in simple search terms. Someone might say, “My family might not be able to pay rent this month,” instead of knowing to search for “rental assistance,” “eviction prevention,” or “emergency housing aid.”

New Leaf uses AI to understand a person’s real situation, match them with relevant support categories, explain eligibility in simple language, and generate a step-by-step action plan.

**AI capabilities used:**  
Natural Language Processing · Recommendation Systems · Personalized Guidance

## Responsible AI

**Risk:**  
The application may recommend incorrect, outdated, or irrelevant community resources. It may also fail to recognize urgent situations where immediate human assistance is needed, potentially delaying critical help for vulnerable users.

**How we reduce it:**  
We use verified and regularly updated resource databases whenever possible. When information is limited or uncertain, the application clearly labels that uncertainty instead of presenting the answer as guaranteed. For urgent situations, New Leaf prioritizes official hotlines, emergency services, and human support instead of relying only on AI-generated guidance.

We also hope to work with government agencies, nonprofits, and trusted community organizations so that they can help vet resources and improve reliability.

## Human in the Loop

Humans remain involved in final decisions about eligibility, applications, and urgent circumstances such as housing loss, food insecurity, financial hardship, or mental health crises.

The AI does not replace social workers, government agencies, emergency services, or trained support workers. It helps users organize their situation, understand possible options, and prepare to ask for help.
